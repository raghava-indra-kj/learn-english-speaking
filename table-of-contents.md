# Corporate English Fluency in 30 Days — From First Principles to Confident Speaker
*A self-verifying, step-by-step plan for working professionals: about one hour a day, every topic broken into Learn → Practice → Verify, nothing left to chance. By Day 30 you speak clearly in meetings, write clean emails, and walk into a job interview with confidence.*

## Who this is for
You go to an office (or want to). You can read basic English and follow simple conversations, but speaking still feels slow — you translate in your head, you hesitate, and you are not always sure your grammar is right. This plan takes you from *"I understand more than I can say"* to *"I can say what I mean, clearly, on the spot."* No specialist background is assumed. Every example comes from ordinary work life: meetings, emails, deadlines, projects, clients, managers, and interviews.

## How to use this map
*The single discipline that makes the 30 days actually work.*

- **The Loop — never skip it.** For every small skill: **Learn** it → **▸ Practice** it (out loud or in writing) → **✓ Checkpoint** (clear the stated bar) → only then move on. Fail a checkpoint? Repeat the topic before advancing.
- **▸ Practice** means active production — speaking, recording, or writing real sentences of your own. Re-reading does **not** count as practice.
- **✓ Checkpoint** is the exact, observable bar you must clear before the next topic *inside the same chapter*.
- **✓ Day Gate** is the end-of-chapter mastery test. Pass it before you start the next chapter. This is how you *see your progress every single day*.
- ***(record)*** means capture it on your phone and listen back — your own recording is the feedback loop that shows you what to fix.
- Each day is one **Chapter** (Day = Chapter = Topic). Each chapter's lesson lives in its own folder under `data/`, as `data/day-001-the-mental-model/content.md` — a three-digit day number in the kebab-case folder name, and the lesson file always named `content.md`.
- **Tier 1 (Days 1–12)** makes you productive and confident across about 80% of everyday workplace communication. **Tier 2 (Days 13–30)** is the remaining 20% — the precision and polish that make you sound genuinely fluent and interview-ready.

*Asking clearly, and making polite professional requests.*

*The one rule behind every English question.*

#### 6.2.3 ✓ Checkpoint — Each keeps the correct inversion after the WH-word

### 6.3 Tag Questions — Confirming, Not Asking
*The "…isn't it?" pattern for checking shared understanding.*
#### 6.3.1 Learn — Positive stem → negative tag: "It's approved, isn't it?"

## Chapter 10 · Day 10 — Meetings + Giving and Receiving Feedback
*The two most-repeated workplace conversations.*

*Three sentences, no more.*
#### 10.1.1 Learn — Max 3 sentences: Done / Doing / Blockers
#### 10.1.2 Learn — Done: "I finished [specific deliverable]."

### 10.2 The 5 Meeting Anti-Patterns
*The exact phrases to delete, and their replacements.*
#### 10.2.1 Learn — "I have completed the task" → "I finished the task."

## Chapter 24 · Day 24 — Stress, Rhythm, and Connected Speech
*Grammar and vocabulary are wasted if people can't easily follow your delivery.*

### 24.1 Stress-Timed Rhythm
*The fundamental break from syllable-timed languages.*
#### 24.1.1 Learn — English stresses at regular intervals; many Indian languages give every syllable equal weight
#### 24.1.2 Learn — Why equal-weight syllables sound flat and choppy to native ears
#### 24.1.3 ▸ Practice — Tap the beat on stressed syllables in 5 sentences
#### 24.1.4 ✓ Checkpoint — Stresses fall on a regular pulse

### 24.2 Content Words vs Function Words
*Stress the meaning-carriers, swallow the rest.*
#### 24.2.1 Learn — Stress content words (nouns, main verbs, adjectives, adverbs, question words)
#### 24.2.2 Learn — Reduce function words (a→/ə/, the→/ðə/, to→/tə/, and→/ən/)
#### 24.2.3 ▸ Practice — Mark stress/reduction in "I'll send the report to the client tonight"
#### 24.2.4 ✓ Checkpoint — Function words are audibly compressed

### 24.3 The 15 Most Mispronounced Common Words
*The words that quietly out you on the first call.*
#### 24.3.1 Learn — colleague, schedule, comfortable, Wednesday, February, genuine, suite

#### 24.3.3 ▸ Practice — Say all 15 correctly *(record)*
#### 24.3.4 ✓ Checkpoint — Each matches the reference pronunciation

### 24.4 Noun vs Verb Stress Pairs
*Same spelling, different stress, different word.*
#### 24.4.1 Learn — RECord/reCORD, PROgress/proGRESS, OBject/obJECT, CONflict/conFLICT, PERmit/perMIT
#### 24.4.2 ▸ Practice — Say each pair in a noun sentence and a verb sentence
#### 24.4.3 ✓ Checkpoint — Stress shifts correctly with the part of speech

### 24.5 Connected Speech Patterns
*How words blend in real native speech.*
#### 24.5.1 Learn — Linking: "signed-off," "filled-in," "checked-in"
#### 24.5.2 Learn — Reduction: gonna, wanna, gotta, hafta, lemme, gimme

*The daily drill that rebuilds your rhythm.*

## A note on how each chapter is taught
Every chapter above is turned into a full lesson by the **english-lesson-builder** skill. Each lesson is written in clear, flowing prose (not choppy fragments), defines every term in plain words, explains the *reasoning* behind each rule, uses generic office examples anyone can follow, and ends with two things you can rely on: a short list of what you can now do, and a quick-summary cheat sheet you can re-read in sixty seconds. The structure flexes to fit the topic — a tense chapter, a pronunciation chapter, and an interview chapter are not forced into an identical mould — but the teacher's voice and the Learn → Practice → Verify discipline stay the same across all thirty days.
